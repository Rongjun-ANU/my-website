About

I am a PhD student at the International Centre for Radio Astronomy Research (ICRAR), The University of Western Australia (2025–2029). My research focuses on galaxy evolution, spatially-resolved star formation, interstellar medium diagnostics, and environmental effects in clusters.

Current focus: Virgo Cluster galaxies, MAUVE-MUSE integral-field spectroscopy, HST UV-optical imaging, and the mass-metallicity relation's dependence on star formation rate surface density.

Research

My PhD work asks how dense environments change galaxies internally: where star formation is suppressed or enhanced, how ISM conditions respond, and how metallicity behaves across individual spaxels instead of only in whole-galaxy averages. Supervisor: Prof. Luca Cortese.

IFS Datacubes

Turning modern integral-field spectroscopy into spatial maps of emission lines, stellar populations, gas diagnostics, and radial structure.

Star Formation

Tracing the resolved star-forming main sequence, SFR surface density, HII regions, and where diagnostics such as H alpha can mislead.

Virgo Environment

Studying cluster-driven transformation in late-type Virgo galaxies using MAUVE-MUSE, HST imaging, and multi-wavelength context.

01 datacube MUSE/IFS spectra sampled across the galaxy, one spaxel at a time.
02 maps Resolved star formation, gas excitation, metallicity, and ISM structure.
03 physics Mass-metallicity relation, SFR surface density, feedback, and outflows.
04 environment How the Virgo Cluster reshapes disks, star clusters, associations, and HII regions.
